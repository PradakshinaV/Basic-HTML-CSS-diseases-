FEVER AND COLD

A fever is a rise in body temperature due to infection, while a cold is a viral infection of the respiratory tract causing congestion, sneezing, and a runny nose.Thses are the most common symptoms of upper respiratory infections, primarily caused by viral infections like rhinoviruses or influenza.

SYMPTOMS:

  • High body temperature (above 100.4°F or 38°C), chills, sweating, headache, fatigue, muscle aches, and weakness.
  • Nasal congestion, runny nose, sneezing, sore throat, mild cough, watery eyes, and occasionally low-grade fever.

CAUSES:

  • Viral infections (e.g., common cold, flu, COVID-19)
  • Exposure to allergens or irritants

MANAGEMENT:

  • Rest and hydration
  • Over-the-counter medications (e.g., acetaminophen for fever, decongestants for cold)
  • Warm fluids like soup or tea
  • Saline nasal sprays to ease congestion
