TUBERCULOSIS

Tuberculosis (TB) is a serious infectious disease primarily affecting the lungs, caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis . It spreads through the air when a person with active TB coughs or sneezes.Treatment involves a combination of antibiotics taken for several months.

SYMPTOMS:

  • Prolonged Cough: A cough that lasts 3 weeks or longer is a key symptom of TB.
  • Unexplained Weight Loss: Significant weight loss without any other obvious reasons can be a sign of active TB.

CAUSES:

  • Caused by bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis.
  • Spread Through the Air when a person with active TB disease coughs or sneezes, releasing tiny droplets containing the bacteria.
MANAGEMENT:
  • Active TB: Requires a combination of antibiotics taken for 6-12 months.
  • Latent TB Infection: May require preventive treatment with antibiotics.
  • Directly Observed Therapy (DOT) may be recommended, where a healthcare worker observes the patient taking medication.
