TYPES OF DISEASES:

  • Infectious Diseases:
    Caused by pathogens like bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites. Examples include:
    • Bacterial: Tuberculosis, cholera.
    • Viral: Influenza, COVID-19.
    • Parasitic: Malaria, giardiasis.

  • Non-Infectious Diseases:
    Not caused by pathogens, often linked to genetics, lifestyle, or environment. Examples:
    • Chronic Diseases: Diabetes, hypertension.
    • Genetic Disorders: Down syndrome, sickle cell anemia.
    • Lifestyle Diseases: Obesity, cardiovascular diseases.
    • Degenerative Diseases: Alzheimer's, Parkinson's.

  • Autoimmune Diseases:
    Occur when the immune system attacks the body's own cells. Examples: Rheumatoid arthritis, lupus.

  • Deficiency Diseases:
    Caused by a lack of essential nutrients. Examples: Scurvy (vitamin C), Rickets (vitamin D).

MODES OF TRANSMISSION:

  • Direct Transmission:
    Person-to-person contact (e.g., touching, coughing, sneezing).
  • Indirect Transmission:
    Through contaminated surfaces, water, or food.
  • Vector-Borne:
    Spread by carriers like mosquitoes (e.g., dengue, malaria).
