MALARIA

Malaria is a life-threatening mosquito-borne disease caused by parasites of the Plasmodium genus. It is transmitted to people through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.

SYMPTOMS:

  • Fever: Often high and may come and go.
  • Chills: Shaking chills that can precede the fever.
  • Headache: Can be severe.
  • Muscle aches: Body aches and pains.

CAUSES:

  • Malaria is caused by parasites of the genus Plasmodium, which are transmitted to people through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.

MANAGEMENT:

  • Early diagnosis is crucial for effective treatment and preventing severe complications.
  • Treatment involves the use of antimalarial drugs, such as artemisinin-based combination therapies (ACTs), which are the most effective treatments currently available.
  • Mosquito Control, using mosquito nets (treated with insecticide).
